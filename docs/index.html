What it does

Fast, private, and out of your way

A stable menu-bar app — no floating widget, no Dock icon. The assistant appears next to your cursor and disappears when you click away.

⌨️

One global shortcut

Select text anywhere and press ⌘⇧K. The popup opens right by your cursor; click outside or hit Esc to dismiss.

⚡

Quick actions

Explain, Summarize, Rewrite, Fix Grammar, Translate, Improve Tone — or type your own one-off prompt.

🌊

Streaming answers

Responses stream in live via the OpenAI Responses API and render as clean Markdown — headings, lists, code.

📋

Copy · Replace · Insert

Put the answer on the clipboard, or paste it straight back into the exact app and field you were working in.

💬

Keep chatting

Ask follow-ups in the same popup, switch models on the fly, and revisit past interactions in History.

Your key, your data

Under the hood

How it works

Four steps, all local except the one call to OpenAI.

Select

Highlight text in any macOS app — a doc, email, browser, or code editor.

Trigger

Press ⌘⇧K. The app copies your selection, then restores your original clipboard.

Ask

Pick an action or type a prompt. The request streams to OpenAI with your key.

Act

Read the Markdown answer, then Copy, Replace, or Insert it back where you were.

Setup · about 2 minutes, once

Install & get started

It isn't distributed through the App Store, so the very first launch needs one manual "open anyway" step. After that it just works.

1

Download & move to Applications

Grab the DMG, open it, and drag FloatingAI.app onto the Applications shortcut.

↓ Download FloatingAI.dmg

2

Allow it past Gatekeeper (first launch only)

Double-click the app. If macOS says it can't verify the developer, open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway. Or run this in Terminal:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/FloatingAI.app
3

Add your OpenAI key

On first run, paste your key (create one at platform.openai.com/api-keys). It's saved in your Keychain — only on your Mac.

4

Grant Accessibility & restart

The app needs Accessibility permission to read your selection and paste answers back. Turn on FloatingAI in Privacy & Security → Accessibility, then click Restart Floating AI.

Tip: a running Mac app can't see a new Accessibility grant until it restarts — that's exactly what the "Restart Floating AI" button is for.
✓

You're set

Look for the ✦ icon in your menu bar. Select any text and press ⌘⇧K.

Privacy by design

Your text and key never touch a middleman

  • ✓ Your OpenAI API key is stored in the macOS Keychain on your device — never uploaded anywhere.
  • ✓ Requests go directly to OpenAI over HTTPS. There is no Floating AI server in the middle.
  • ✓ History is a plain JSON file in your own Application Support folder; clear it anytime.
  • ✓ Accessibility permission is used only to read your current selection and paste responses back — nothing else.
  • ✓ Open source — read exactly what it does on GitHub.
Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need an OpenAI account?
Yes — Floating AI uses your own OpenAI API key so you stay in control of usage and billing. Create a key at platform.openai.com/api-keys and paste it on first launch.
What does it cost?
The app is free. You only pay OpenAI for the tokens your requests consume, billed to your own account — typically a fraction of a cent per action with GPT-4o mini.
Is my data private?
Your key is stored in the macOS Keychain and requests go straight to OpenAI over HTTPS. Nothing is proxied through any third-party server, and your history stays on your Mac.
Why the "can't verify developer" warning?
Because the app is distributed outside the App Store and isn't notarized by Apple. It's safe — use Open Anyway in System Settings → Privacy & Security, or run xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/FloatingAI.app. One time only.
Why does it need Accessibility permission?
To read the text you've selected (via Copy) and paste answers back (via Paste) into other apps. macOS gates that behind Accessibility. It's used for nothing else, and you can revoke it anytime.
It keeps asking for permission / the shortcut does nothing.
Grant Accessibility to FloatingAI, then click Restart Floating AI — a running app only sees a new permission after it restarts. If ⌘⇧K is taken by another app, toggle or change it in Settings → General, or open from the menu-bar ✦.
Does it work on Intel Macs?
Yes. The download is a universal build (Apple Silicon + Intel) and runs on macOS 13 Ventura or later.
Where's the app icon? I don't see it in the Dock.
By design there's no Dock icon — Floating AI lives in the menu bar as the ✦ icon, which holds Open Assistant, Settings, History, and Quit.
Can I build it myself?
Yes — it's open source and builds with the Xcode Command Line Tools (no full Xcode). See the repository: ./run.sh to build & launch, ./package.sh to produce the DMG.
