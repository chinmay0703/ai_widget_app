What it does

Fast, private, out of your way

A stable menu-bar app — no floating widget, no Dock icon. The assistant appears next to your cursor and disappears when you click away.

⌨️

One shortcut

Select text anywhere and press ⌘⇧K. The popup opens right by your cursor; click outside to dismiss.

⚡

Quick actions

Explain, Summarize, Rewrite, Fix Grammar, Translate, Improve Tone — or type your own prompt.

🌊

Streaming answers

Responses stream in live via the OpenAI Responses API, rendered as clean Markdown.

📋

Copy · Replace · Insert

Put the answer on the clipboard, or paste it straight back into the app you were working in.

🔐

Bring your own key

💬

Keep chatting

Ask follow-ups in the same popup, pick any model, and review past interactions in History.

Setup · about 2 minutes, once

Install & get started

It isn't distributed through the App Store, so the very first launch needs one manual "open anyway" step. After that it just works.

1

Download & move to Applications

Grab the DMG, open it, and drag FloatingAI.app onto the Applications shortcut.

↓ Download FloatingAI.dmg

2

Allow it past Gatekeeper (first launch only)

Double-click the app. If macOS says it can't verify the developer, open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway. Or run this in Terminal:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/FloatingAI.app
3

Add your OpenAI key

On first run, paste your key (create one at platform.openai.com/api-keys). It's saved in your Keychain — only on your Mac.

4

Grant Accessibility & restart

The app needs Accessibility permission to read your selection and paste answers back. Turn on FloatingAI in Privacy & Security → Accessibility, then click Restart Floating AI.

Tip: a running Mac app can't see a new Accessibility grant until it restarts — that's what the "Restart Floating AI" button is for.
✓

You're set

Look for the ✦ icon in your menu bar. Select any text and press ⌘⇧K.

Everyday use

How to use it

  1. Select text in any app — a doc, browser, email, code.
  2. Press ⌘⇧K (or click the menu-bar ✦ → Open Assistant).
  3. Pick a quick action or type a prompt, then send.
  4. Use Copy, Replace, or Insert to put the answer back.
  5. Ask follow-ups in the same popup, or press Esc to close.
The ✦ menu-bar icon holds Settings (model, system prompt, temperature, shortcut), History, and Quit. There's no Dock icon by design.

Good to know

Requirements & FAQ

💻

Requirements

macOS 13 (Ventura) or later, on Apple Silicon or Intel. An OpenAI API key. ~0.8 MB download.

🔒

Is my key safe?

Yes — it's stored in the macOS Keychain on your Mac and sent only in direct requests to OpenAI. Nothing is proxied through a server.

🛡️

"Can't verify" warning?

Normal for apps outside the App Store. Use Open Anyway in Privacy & Security, or the xattr command in step 2. One time only.

⌨️

Shortcut not firing?

Another app may own ⌘⇧K, or it's off in Settings. Toggle it in the app's Settings → General, or open from the menu-bar ✦.

🧩

No menu-bar icon?

The app may not be running — relaunch it. There's no Dock icon by design; the ✦ icon is the app.

💸

What does it cost?

The app is free. You pay OpenAI only for the tokens your requests use, billed to your own account.
